The film is about The Battle of Stalingrad. It focuses on a prolonged sniper duel and touches on the battle's importance to Soviet moral. The Red Army's victory is considered the turning point in the war on the eastern front. It marked the end of Germany's initial advance into Russia and set the stage for a Soviet offensive. While being one of the bloodiest battles in recorded history, Stalingrad was the first time in the war where the Red Army was able to make significant gains in German held territory. Isaak Budnitsky, Efim Perlitch, Grigory Genin, Danil Zharnitsky, Alexi Shtern, and Iosif Zibenberg fought in the battle.

wikipedia.org

The real life Vasiley Zaitsev fought at Stalingrad. He had 257 confirmed sniper kills and was featured in propaganda.

The movie angered many veterans of the actual battle. They took issue with the implication that Red Army soldiers were undisciplined and forced to fight. Many felt that the plot about 2 snipers ignores the close quartered combat that actually characterized the battle. They were also unhappy that the movie only touches on Stalingrad's harsh winter conditions.

Lyudmila Palichenko (above) could be the real life basis for the Tania character. Palichenko was at Stalingrad and had 300 sniper kills.

Khrushchev was at Stalingrad but acted more as a political liaison than military strategist. He was involved in selecting Zaitsev for a propaganda campaign, focusing on examples of individual heroism.
